Grocery Trip

Written and illustrated by Leah Faulkner

Three little girls sat on the porch, wishing there was something fun to do. Little Connie had come to play for the afternoon at the McNamara house. Sisters Nancy and Pam loved to play with Connie, even though she could be a little mischievous at times.


“Let’s help out and do the grocery shopping!”

Connie exclaimed in sudden excitement. The McNamara girls nodded enthusiastically. Nancy and Pam snuck into the kitchen, grabbed their mom’s purse, and pulled out her wallet. Inside was exactly what they needed: money. When they got back to the porch, they were ready for begin their adventure!



a sight it was, to see three little girls in such a dangerous setting. Nevertheless, they pushed on, hanging a right on the next street, and walking several yards further. Finally, they see what they have been searching for. The grocery store is just up ahead!



Pam grabbed a cart and wheeled it into the store. They waltzed into the store and began inspecting all the different aisles. Connie and Nancy hauled a watermelon into the cart. They got all the essentials: chips and Juicy Fruit gum.



At the checkout, the cashier smiled down at the girls with a questioning look. They piled the groceries onto the conveyer belt. Nancy offered their mother's wallet to the cashier. They finished the transaction and took their bags of groceries. 

The girls began their journey home. Nancy rolled the watermelon on the ground as they crossed the highway again.  When they got back into the house, they unpacked their groceries. However, Mom soon discovered the goods from the grocery trip. Nancy and Pam got a big scolding, and Connie was sent home for the day. 



The girls weren’t allowed to attend the State Fair that weekend. However, they did get to munch on some fresh watermelon!

The End
